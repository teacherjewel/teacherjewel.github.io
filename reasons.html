There are many ups and downs to every career, which makes it hard to choose. The idea of missed oppurtunities or mistakes can be frightening, and we have to make this decision very early in our lives. Luckily we know what we're good at, and have an idea of what we enjoy by the time we enter our 20's. In the event that teacher is one of your possibilties, hopefully this will prove helpful.

As you can see on the education page, there's a lot of schooling going towards this career which means a lot of possible student debt. There are ways around student debt, but this isn't the page for that. You're going to be a government employee with what I consider one of the most important jobs you can have; educating the future generations. Down below I'll list what I consider pros and cons, you don't need to agree with them.

Pros

  1. Summers off
  2. Thanksgiving break, Winter break, and Spring break off
  3. Constant pay with salary
  4. Keeps you active in the community
  5. You get to work with kids
  6. Helps you become better at teaching, a useful parenting skill
  7. Helps ensure you continue to use your brain to learn throughout your life
  8. There are a lot of options for age group and subject so you aren't majorly restricted
  9. Good hours, 7-3 on weekdays
  10. Great retirement plan
  11. Your job is very important, know that your work does make a difference

Cons

  1. There is a lot of schooling involved
  2. The pay is low for the amount of schooling
  3. You do work some holidays students get off
  4. Work will often come home as grading and planning takes time
  5. Kids are rude and won't always appreciate the effort you put in for them
  6. Meetings
  7. There are several ages and subjects to choose from, so it can be hard to choose
